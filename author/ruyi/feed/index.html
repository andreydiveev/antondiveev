The post ‘Straps/ Strings/ Stitches’ – a Collection by Ewelina Jastrzębska appeared first on Pigeons & Peacocks.

]]> Ewelina’s menswear collection has been designed around the motive to install new meaning to what we think of beauty and perfection.

The clothes used in this shoot were inspired by human body and it’s imperfections such as wounds, scars and medical stitches. The main goal was to use their unaesthetic values and emphasise them through irregular stitching, loose strings or dyeing. Through this process the goal was to enhance these aspects of the body into forms pleasing to the eye, persuading us to embrace the human body as it is, without alterations or “upgrades”.

The post ‘Metamorflosses’: A Photographic Critique on Biotechnologies appeared first on Pigeons & Peacocks.

]]> Julie Tixier‘s photographic practice explores the way human nature is changing under the influence of emerging techno-sciences. In her series ‘Metamorflosses’ the artist reflects on the growing creation of artificial organs and tissues with the advances of biotechnologies. She manipulates candy floss, through its capacity for change, as a metaphor for the transformation of an organic matter to an artificial one. As the sugar filaments of candy floss are similar to the very thin capillary systems constituting organs, the artist mimics life-sized artificial tissues and other essentiel organs. By reinventing the scientific language with a non-scientific substance she questions the experimental conditions of contemporary scientific research. She interlaces scientific methodology and fictional interpretation, blurring the boundaries between the organic and the synthetic. By doing so, the artist draws attention to complex issues in a satirical and playful language.

The post Atrament- A Collection by You Mi Choi appeared first on Pigeons & Peacocks.

]]> LCF’s BA Fashion Textiles: Embroidery student You Mi Choi showcases a collection with the attention to colourful intricate detail and silhouette. 
Within the kaleidoscopic pattern of the Chartres Cathedral’s western rose window is an age old artist expression of the Last Judgment. It speaks of finality and eternality, a line that leaves no space for turning back once crossed. The fashion world is bound by unspoken rules and boundaries, unseen lines that designers work desperately to cross every day. This collection takes inspiration from the window and pushes the once sacred image into an expression of individualism. The dress departs from conventional standards of ready to wear fashion, challenging our modern senses by mixing in medieval and gothic elements. It serves as a reminder that even as we move into a dizzying age of technological sophistication where everything seems possible with the touch of our fingertips, a part of us will always be shadowed by the past, caged in by the grills of our self imposed conventions.
