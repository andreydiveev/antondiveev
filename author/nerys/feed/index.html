]]> LCF graduate Julie El-Bacha presents her collection 

‘Contemporary Ophelia began with John Everett Millais’ interpretation on Shakespeare’s doomed character. The aesthetic of the painting is overwhelmingly beautiful, and yet it tells a morbid story of Ophelia’s ‘muddy death’.’

The sinister beauty of this painting was a concept Julie wanted her collection to embody. Millais’ masterpiece spurred her intrigue into pre-Raphaelite painting and so she studied various artists’ work that also recreated a similar scene such as Krzysztof Macikowski and Slevin Aaron.
